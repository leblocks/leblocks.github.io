Lazy eye blocks

What is this?

This game is intended to help people with "Lazy eye" condition train their weaker eye. Amblyopia, commonly known as lazy eye, is the eye condition noted by reduced vision not correctable by glasses or contact lenses and is not due to any eye disease. The brain, for some reason, does not fully acknowledge the images seen by the amblyopic eye. This almost always affects only one eye but may manifest with reduction of vision in both eyes. It is estimated that three percent of children under six have some form of amblyopia.

How does this work?

Recent studies have shown that playing blocks games in a dihoptic way (when eyes are seeing different images) requires information to be sent to both eyes, making them work cooperatively. By making both eyes cooperate, the amblyopic brain is able to relearn as a result of an increase in the level of plasticity in the brain.

How to use?

Before you begin, consult with your eye doctor

Before you start

You will need anaglyph glasses you don't need to have exactly red\blue glasses, colors are adjustable in this game. You can buy them almost everywhere on the internet (try aliexpress, ebay or amazon)

Instructions

General info:

  1. It is recommended (check this with your eye doctor) to play the game from 20 to 60 minutes a day in order to achieve some noticeable effect. It is possible to split this time into two or three sessions a day
  2. Don't forget to rest and give your brain time to adapt
  3. It is easier to start with a smaller screen first. The number of rows and columns of game field is adjustable, so you can play on various kinds of screens
  4. If you feel it is too easy or too hard for you, you can try switching colors between your healthy and your lazy eye

Color setup:

  1. Click 'settings' button. The settings menu will open
  2. Put up your anaglyph glasses
  3. Click 'New game' button, now you should see game field with first falling block (by default it will be in red color)
  4. Press space to drop it, now you should see first dropped block (ground block) it will be in blue color by default
  5. Click 'Pause' or press 'p', and you will be able to adjust colors
  6. Click on 'falling blocks' color picker (white rectangle under 'Falling blocks color:' label) you will see colorpicker, Once you clicked and changed color of colorpicker the color of falling block will immediately start to change. Now, close your healthy eye and try to adjust the color of the falling block, so it would completely match the color of background. In other words, the falling block must be visible for your healthy eye and almost (better completely) unvisible for your lazy eye
  7. Adjust the color of the 'ground blocks' in the same manner, so it would be visible for your lazy eye and almost (better completely) invisible for your healthy eye
  8. After you completed adjusting process, you are able to save color settings with 'save' button. Next time you enter the game, the color settings will be loaded automatically

Board size setup:

  1. Click 'settings' button, you should see settings menu open
  2. Scroll down to 'Column count' and 'Row count' inputs, here you can put desired number of rows and column
  3. Remember: game will always try to fit height of the board to the screen, so too many rows leads to very small grid size
  4. Click 'set' to start new game, these settings won't be saved, because wrong setup can lead to unpleasant image
  5. Play with this settings until you find the most suitable for your needs

Keyboard controls:

  1. UP arrow - rotate
  2. DOWN, LEFT, RIGHT arrow keys - basic movements
  3. SPACE - fire down current shape
  4. press 'p' for pause
  5. press 'g' for grid
